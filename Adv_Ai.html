II. Course Information

Course: Advanced Artificial Intelligence — 0602443

Prerequisite: 0602341 Artificial Intelligence

Course Description: This course covers advanced topics in artificial intelligence (AI) including planning, expert systems, fuzzy logic, nature-inspired algorithms (evolutionary computation and swarm intelligence), and learning from observations. Students will gain a firm grounding in techniques and component areas of AI and be able to apply this knowledge to develop intelligent systems.

Instructional Time

Additional instruction is provided through Moodle (recorded lecture videos, supplementary videos, discussion boards, and announcements/email).

Student Learning Outcomes

  1. Explain possible designs for implementing intelligent agents
  2. Describe different definitions and tests for AI
  3. Use DFS, BFS, Iterative Deepening, and A* to implement goal-oriented agents
  4. Describe heuristic search strategies and algorithms
  5. Use local search (hill climbing, simulated annealing, genetic algorithms)
  6. Use minimax, expectiminimax, and alpha/beta pruning for multiagent environments
  7. Develop knowledge-based agents using logical systems
  8. Implement agents that handle uncertainty using Probability and Utility Theory
  9. Use Bayesian Networks to Moodle and solve problems
  10. Describe how intelligent agents learn using machine learning algorithms

Program & Graduate Outcomes

  • Understand core principles of intelligent agents
  • Implement and evaluate search algorithms
  • Analyze AI system performance and trade-offs
  • Utilize ML algorithms and address overfitting and evaluation

GSLO1: Synthesize theoretical and research concepts.
GSLO2: Formulate creative responses to complex issues.

VI. Grading Criteria & Course Policies

Course Requirements

  • Readings & lecture videos: Watch required lecture videos and read textbook sections each week. Failure to watch recorded lectures can result in loss of credit for that week's assignments.
  • Quizzes: Weeks 1–7. Multiple choice / true-false. 60 minutes each. You may retake a quiz up to two times; highest score counts.
  • Machine Problems (Programming): Implement AI solutions in Python or specified software. Descriptions posted on Blackboard. Late assignments will not be accepted — late = zero. Submissions must be honest attempts; plagiarized or irrelevant submissions receive zero.
  • Comprehensive Final Exam: Given during the final week in class. Based on lectures, readings, quizzes, and machine problems. You may take the final once.

Course Grade

Average of Machine Problems: 20%
Midterm Exam: 30%
Final Exam: 50%

Policies

  • Turn in all assignments and take all scheduled tests by the due date. Extensions and make-up tests are not given except for special circumstances communicated promptly by email.
  • Under normal circumstances, late assignments receive zero.
  • Academic Honesty: Copying from other students, online sources (including generative AI), or past students results in zero for the assignment. Repeat offenses may result in failing the course.
  • Check Moodle regularly for announcements and materials. Lecture videos, assignments, and other course materials are posted on Moodle. All submissions must be via Moodle assignment area.

Getting Help & How to Succeed

If you need help, contact the instructor by email (listed above) or visit office hours. Start assignments early and seek help when stuck. I will normally reply to email within 24 hours.
